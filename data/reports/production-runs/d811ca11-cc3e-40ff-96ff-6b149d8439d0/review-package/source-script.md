# Why Car Engines Overheat Even With Coolant

## Hook

A full coolant tank does not guarantee a cool engine. The liquid must keep moving and release its heat.

## Main Story

Start with a close-up cutaway of the thermostat. If this small valve stays shut, hot coolant remains trapped inside the engine.

Next, the water pump must push coolant through the engine block. A damaged impeller can spin without moving enough liquid.

At the radiator, narrow passages and moving air carry heat away. Dirt, internal blockage, or a failed fan reduces that escape route.

## Warning

When the dashboard gauge climbs, continuing to drive can warp the cylinder head and turn a small cooling fault into a major repair.

## Outro

Stop safely, switch off the engine, and let it cool. Never open a hot pressurized cooling system.
